Phishing is a cyberattack where criminals send fraudulent communications—typically via
email, WhatsApp, or SMS—that appear to come from a reputable source. The goal is to
steal sensitive data like login credentials, credit card numbers, or Aadhaar details. This
case study explores a targeted "Spear Phishing" attack on a final-year engineering student.
How it Happens :
1. Reconnaissance: The attacker scrapes the student’s name and college details from
LinkedIn or a public placement coordinator’s list.
2. The Lure: The student receives an email that looks exactly like a formal oƯer from a
top-tier firm (e.g., Google or TCS), oƯering a "Remote Summer Internship" with a
high stipend
3. The Hook: To "confirm the oƯer," the student is asked to click a link to a "Candidate
Portal." This link leads to a perfectly cloned website that looks identical to the
company’s actual login page.
4. The Harvest: When the student "registers" using their Gmail or University
credentials, the attacker captures their username and password in real-time.
5. The Exploitation: The attacker uses these credentials to access the student’s
primary email, which is linked to their bank account and social media, leading to a
total account takeover.
Typical Targets: Students actively seeking jobs or internships are primary targets. Their
eagerness for career opportunities makes them more likely to overlook red flags like a
slightly misspelled email domain (e.g., hr@googIe.com with a capital 'I' instead of 'l').
